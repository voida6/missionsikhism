## A birth remembered two ways

Nanak was born into a Khatri family in the village of Rai Bhoi Ki Talwandi, south-west of Lahore, to Mehta Kalu, the village accountant, and his wife Tripta. The janam-sakhi birth narratives place his birth in the month of Vaisakh (April), yet his Gurpurab is traditionally observed on the full moon of Kartik (October–November) — a divergence the historical sources note without resolving. The year 1469 is accepted across the scholarly literature.

## Early signs

The janam-sakhis relate a childhood marked by precocious spiritual insight: the boy who questioned the sacred-thread ceremony, the schoolboy who turned his lessons toward the divine. Historians treat these stories as devotional tradition rather than verifiable record, but they preserve what the earliest community valued in its founder — a refusal to accept ritual without meaning. His elder sister, Bebe Nanaki, is remembered as the first to recognise his calling.

## The village becomes a city of pilgrimage

Talwandi was later renamed [Nankana Sahib](/places/nankana-sahib) in the Guru's honour. Gurdwara Janam Asthan now marks the traditional birthplace, and the town — in Pakistan since the [Partition of 1947](/events/partition-of-punjab) — remains one of the faith's most visited pilgrimage sites, thronged each year at Gurpurab. His birth begins [Era 1 — the Guru Period](/history/guru-period), and his revelation [at Sultanpur](/events/enlightenment-at-sultanpur) some thirty years later begins the faith itself.
