## A purchase in the foothills

In 1665, in the first year of his Guruship, Guru Tegh Bahadur bought land near the village of Makhowal, on high ground above the Sutlej beneath the Naina Devi hills. The new settlement was named Chakk Nanaki, after his mother; the town that grew from it took the name Anandpur — "city of bliss." The purchase gave the community something it had lacked: a seat of its own, away from the contested towns of the plains and defensible by geography.

## Refuge and capital

It was to Anandpur in 1675 that the Kashmiri Brahmins came to seek the Guru's intercession against forced conversion — the appeal that led to [his martyrdom in Delhi](/events/martyrdom-of-guru-tegh-bahadur). His son [Guru Gobind Singh](/people/guru-gobind-singh) made the town his capital for most of his Guruship: a centre of poetry and learning as much as arms, ringed in time with forts — Anandgarh, Keshgarh, Lohgarh, Fatehgarh among them.

## Cradle of the Khalsa

A generation after its founding, the town witnessed the faith's defining assembly: on Vaisakhi 1699 the Khalsa was [created here](/events/creation-of-the-khalsa), at the spot marked today by Takht Sri Keshgarh Sahib, one of the faith's five Takhts. Besieged repeatedly, [Anandpur](/places/anandpur) was finally evacuated under promise of safe conduct in 1705 — a promise broken at the Sarsa river with consequences the tradition mourns to this day. The town remains the great gathering place of Hola Mahalla, the martial festival the tenth Guru instituted.
