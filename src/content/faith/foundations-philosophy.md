## One reality

Sikh philosophy begins with **Ik Onkar** — there is one God, one universal Creator, formless and beyond birth or death. This is the opening statement of the Mool Mantar, the first verse of the [Guru Granth Sahib](/faith/one-god), and the foundation on which everything else rests.

## Three pillars

Guru Nanak set out a way of life resting on three practices: **Naam Japna** (remembrance of God through meditation and prayer), **Kirat Karni** (earning an honest living), and **Vand Chakna** (sharing what one has with others). Devotion is meant to be lived in the world, not withdrawn from it.

## Equality and service

Sikhi rejects distinctions of caste, birth and gender before God. The institution of **langar** — the free community kitchen where all sit and eat together as equals — makes that belief visible. Alongside it stand **seva** (selfless service) and **sarbat da bhala** (the welfare of all humanity).

## Spiritual and worldly

Guru Hargobind expressed the principle of **miri-piri** — that spiritual and temporal life are inseparable. A Sikh is called to inner devotion and to just, engaged action in the world alike.
