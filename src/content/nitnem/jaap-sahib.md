## What it is

Jaap Sahib opens the Dasam Granth, the collection associated with [Guru Gobind Singh](/people/guru-gobind-singh), and runs to some hundred and ninety-nine verses in swift, drumming metres. Where the [Japji](/nitnem/japji-sahib) meditates, the Jaap salutes: verse upon verse of the attributes of God — in Punjabi, Braj, Sanskrit and Persian vocabulary alike — naming the Nameless from every direction and withdrawing every name: salutation to the One without country, without garb, without form.

## What it teaches

The composition is negative theology at speed — God beyond time, beyond mark, beyond religion's own categories, *Ajapa* (beyond recitation) even as it is recited. Its rhythm carries the tenth Guru's court poetry into prayer; read aloud it moves at a martial cadence the [tradition](/culture/martial-tradition) has always heard as the sound of steel — contemplation fit for a community then learning to defend itself.

## When it is read

Jaap Sahib follows Japji in the morning nitnem and is one of the five banis recited over the vessel [at the amrit ceremony](/faith/ceremonies-and-rites) — the initiate's first inheritance from the Guru who [created the Khalsa](/events/creation-of-the-khalsa).
