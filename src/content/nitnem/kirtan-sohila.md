## What it is

Sohila — the "song of praise" — is the shortest of the daily banis: five hymns, three of [Guru Nanak](/people/guru-nanak) with one each of Guru Ram Das and Guru Arjan, gathered near the [Guru Granth Sahib's](/faith/guru-granth-sahib) opening. Its central image is among the most loved in the scripture: the *aarti* hymn in which the sky is the salver, sun and moon the lamps, the stars its pearls — all creation itself the worship, no platter of flames required.

## What it faces

This is the prayer with which Sikhs keep company with death. Its hymns speak of the summons that comes to every house, the wedding-day of the soul, union with the divine as homecoming rather than loss — which is why Sohila is recited not only nightly but at cremations, where its calm reframes [the faith's understanding](/faith/foundations-philosophy) of dying as return.

## When it is read

Kirtan Sohila is recited last thing before sleep, and it is sung as the scripture is ceremonially rested for the night at the Harmandir Sahib [in Amritsar](/places/amritsar) — the same words closing the day of a household and of the faith's centre alike.
