## What it is

Rehras is an anthology rather than a single composition: at its core stand the So Dar and So Purakh clusters — hymns of [Guru Nanak](/people/guru-nanak), Guru Ram Das and Guru Arjan drawn from the [Guru Granth Sahib](/faith/guru-granth-sahib) — joined in practice by compositions of [Guru Gobind Singh](/people/guru-gobind-singh), including the Benti Chaupai's petition for protection, six stanzas of the Anand Sahib, and closing verses. The exact contents have varied by community and era; the [Rehat Maryada](/faith/rehat-maryada) records the standard sequence.

## What it carries

The evening prayer turns on gratitude and steadying: *so dar* asks "what is that gate, what is that house?" where the divine sits sustaining all creation — and answers with a universe in attendance. Where the morning banis brace the day ahead, Rehras gathers its end: thanks for what was given, strength restored after labour, protection sought for the night.

## When it is read

Rehras is recited at dusk — in homes and in every [gurdwara](/glossary/gurdwara), where it precedes the evening settling of the scripture. The day it closes ends fully with [Kirtan Sohila](/nitnem/kirtan-sohila) at bedtime.
