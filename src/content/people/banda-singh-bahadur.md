## The ascetic at Nanded

Born Lachman Dev in 1670 at Rajouri in the Jammu hills, he was a hunter who renounced the world — the tradition says over a doe killed with her fawns — and wandered India as the ascetic Madho Das, settling on the Godavari at [Nanded](/places/nanded). There in 1708 the dying [Guru Gobind Singh](/people/guru-gobind-singh) sought him out. The hermit gave himself as the Guru's *banda* — his slave — and received arms, counsellors, and a commission: justice for the Punjab.

## Six years of sovereignty

What followed [is told in the era](/history/sovereignty-banda-singh) that bears his name: the peasantry in arms, [Sirhind broken at Chappar Chiri](/events/battle-of-chappar-chiri) in 1710, coin and seal struck in the Gurus' names, and — the chronicles suggest — tenancy turned to ownership for the cultivators of his territories. The empire required five years and its best armies to starve him out of Gurdas Nangal.

## The unbroken end

Paraded through [Delhi](/places/delhi) in a cage in 1716, offered life for conversion daily as his companions were executed in their hundreds, he refused to the last and [died under torture in June 1716](/events/martyrdom-of-banda-singh), his small son murdered before him. Mughal and European witnesses alike recorded the prisoners' serenity. The state he improvised did not survive him; the proof that the [Khalsa](/glossary/khalsa) could rule did.
