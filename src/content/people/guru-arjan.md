## Builder of the centre

Youngest son of [Guru Ram Das](/people/guru-ram-das) and Bibi Bhani, Arjan became fifth Guru in 1581 and finished what his father began: the great pool at [Amritsar](/places/amritsar) completed, and at its centre the Harmandir Sahib — built low, the tradition notes, and open on four sides to every caste and quarter. He founded new towns — Tarn Taran, with its refuge for lepers, among them — and regularised the *dasvandh* tithe and masand collectors that bound the scattered congregations to the centre.

## Poet and editor

Guru Arjan is the [Guru Granth Sahib's](/faith/guru-granth-sahib) largest single contributor — over two thousand hymns, including the Sukhmani, the "psalm of peace." His great editorial act was [the Adi Granth of 1604](/events/installation-of-adi-granth): the Gurus' hymns fixed against forgery, arranged by raag, and set alongside the words of Hindu and Muslim saints — then installed in the Harmandir above the Guru himself.

## The first martyr

The faith's growth drew imperial attention. Accused in the affair of the rebel prince Khusrau, the Guru [died under torture in Mughal custody at Lahore in 1606](/events/martyrdom-of-guru-arjan) — the martyrdom that ended the faith's purely pacific age. His son [Hargobind](/people/guru-hargobind) girded [two swords](/glossary/miri-piri), and [the community changed course](/history/guru-period).
