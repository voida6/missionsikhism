## Life

Born at Rai Bhoi Ki Talwandi — now [Nankana Sahib](/places/nankana-sahib) — in [1469](/events/birth-of-guru-nanak), Nanak worked as a steward in Sultanpur Lodhi until, at about thirty, the experience remembered as [his revelation at the river Bein](/events/enlightenment-at-sultanpur) set him on the road. With the Muslim musician Mardana at his side, he travelled for some two decades on the great journeys (*udasis*) the tradition carries east to Assam, south toward Sri Lanka, north into the mountains and west to Mecca and Baghdad — teaching in song, in the language of the people.

## The community at Kartarpur

Around 1522 he settled [at Kartarpur](/events/founding-of-kartarpur) on the Ravi and led the first Sikh community as a farming householder: meditation on the divine Name, honest work, food shared in the common [langar](/glossary/langar) — the pattern of [the faith's foundations](/faith/foundations-philosophy) lived daily. Nearly a thousand of his hymns, including the Japji recited [each morning](/nitnem/japji-sahib), anchor the [Guru Granth Sahib](/faith/guru-granth-sahib), opening with the declaration of [one God](/faith/one-god).

## Succession

Before his death at Kartarpur in 1539 he passed over his sons and installed the disciple Lehna as [Guru Angad](/people/guru-angad) — establishing that the Guruship follows devotion, not descent, and securing the young community's future through [nine further Gurus](/history/guru-period).
